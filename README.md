## 📖 Project Overview

This repository houses the **Final Exam Statistics Project** (`UAS_Statistika.ipynb`) dedicated to calculating, analyzing, and visualizing macroeconomic trade metrics. The analysis centers around **Indonesian Import Performance throughout the year 2024**, utilizing official empirical data sourced from the **Central Bureau of Statistics (BPS Indonesia)**. 

The analytical pipeline applies rigorous descriptive statistical methods to transform raw trade numbers into clear, structural economic insights, evaluating fluctuations, key performance indicators, and trade balances.

## 🎯 Analysis Objectives

The final examination project implements systematic statistical tracking to assess national import patterns:
- 📊 **Descriptive Statistics:** Summarizing central tendencies, structural growth, and distribution of imports across various product sectors.
- 📉 **Volatility & Dispersion Tracking:** Employing statistical variance and standard deviation to analyze monthly trade fluctuations and economic stability.
- 📈 **Trend Visualization:** Translating complex import matrices into intuitive visual line plots and data distribution charts to simplify macroeconomic forecasting.

## 📊 Key Methodology & Metrics

The notebook implements a structured statistical reporting workflow to interpret economic trade variations:

*   📂 **Data Preparation:** Cleans official external trade data sheets by stripping out null parameters, restructuring index tokens, and matching temporal data attributes.
*   📉 **Dispersion Analytics:** Computes the **Standard Deviation** across core import categories throughout the fiscal year 2024 to gauge seasonal or market-driven market volatility inside specific industrial domains.
*   📊 **Macro Trends Reporting:** Evaluates the aggregate national import valuation cycles over consecutive months to map out clear macroeconomic supply line trends.
